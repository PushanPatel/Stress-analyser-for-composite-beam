Introduction

[1]A beam is a structural element that primarily resists transverse loading. We come across various encounter with beam action in our surrounding: [2]horizontal member in building act as a beam in transferring the vertical floor loading to the columns and foundation walls; the leaf spring of an automobile suspension transfer the bodyweight to the axle through-beam action; the wings of an aeroplane act as a beam in supporting the weight of the fuselage.

The primary aim of the module is to determine the distribution of stresses in a beam subjected to pure bending and eccentric loading. This writeup also comprises the stress analysis of composite beam. We will limit our discussion to the straight beam having the cross-section with at least one plane of symmetry. In the straight beam, the axis of beam coincides with one of the co-ordinate axes.

All the forces acting on a beam fall in a plane called plane of loading. We will assume that the plane of loads passes through a point in the beam cross-section so that it will not induce any twisting in the beam[3]. The resulting force that acts on the beam will only produce bending moment and shear forces at any cross-section of the beam, that in turn induces internal stresses, strain and deflection of the beam. We will develop formulas for stresses in a beam subjected to symmetrical bending, i.e. the plane of load will coincide with one of the main axes of the beam cross-section.
