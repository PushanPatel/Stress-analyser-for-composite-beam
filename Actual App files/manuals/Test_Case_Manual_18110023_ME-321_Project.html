LIMITATIONS:

1)For stress inputs beyond the yield strength of the materials, the app will give finite values of stresses & finite distribution. It does not take into account the yielding behaviour.

2)The app can only handle vertically composite beams. It is not designed for horizontally composite beams.

3)The beam should have at least 1 Plane of symmetry (vertical plane).

4)Shear stress & it’s effects are not taken into account anywhere. So the app cannot function for scenarios where shear stress is acting.

5)The app takes the bottom most material as reference and subsequently modifies the other material cross-sections. Hence, for composite beams where the highest modulus material is at the bottom, the app will give non-standard results. Thus, in such cases it cannot be used as a solo transformed section maker.

ASSUMPTIONS:

1)The dimension inputs are all in mm, according to the sign convention & given.

2)The stress input is within the elastic limit of the material.

3)The material property (the modulus of elasticity, is in MPa)

4)The input beam parameters are entered in a Bottom to top order.

5)The user must know how the expected outputs are calculated, and what the symbols & terms mean, as they are provided with supporting theory & help manuals.

TEST CASE NO.: 4

TEST CASE SCENARIO: Sandwich beam subjected to eccentric loading

CONVENTION & THEORY:

The geometrical centre of the composite beam is taken as the centroid and the distance of the axial load applied is taken from there.

The Moment acting on the beam due to the axial load will be:

M = P x d = 22360 Nm
